Creative Brief

Portfolios are a necessary addition for all designers in this modern world. Most, if not all, jobs require a digital website portfolio to be submitted when applying to work. This means that portfolio websites should be well designed and considered as an extension to one’s work.

As a senior graphic designer, I have been able to develop my own branding and logo. For this portfolio, I will incorporate this branding I developed throughout the website. My style is minimal and simple, and the portfolio should reflect that.

Through my research, I found that the best way to catch a client’s attention is by making the website unique to oneself. Combining personal and professional elements to add a personal touch. Adding to this, I learned that simplicity is not just through visual design, but also in terms of user experience. Clients primarily want to understand who I am, what I do, and what I did.

My navigation bar will consist of a home introduction, portfolio/work, resume, and contact. I want my website to be straightforward and easy to use. Each portfolio piece will have explanations behind the process and challenges, a list of programs used, who the client is, and the year it was made.

Objectives

    Represent my style of work through branding and colors
    Clean, consistent style
    Include explanations of my design decisions and challenges I faced
